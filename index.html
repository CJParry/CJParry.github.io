November, 2021

NFT Random Generator

To build on my NFT and blockchain skills, I created a line of randomly generated NFTs, lazy minted from a React front-end. The NFT metadata is generated dynamically and stored in a locally run IPFS node. To allow for true random NFT generation, I used the Ethers.js library to sign the customers transaction with the NFT contract owners private key at runtime.

August - October, 2021

Arbitrage Trading Bot

Written in Solidity and Web3, this bot listens for swap events on various decentralized exchanges, such as UniSwap and PancakeSwap. When the price spread between two pools presents an arbitrage opportunity, it utilises a flash loan to borrow the cheaper asset and sell it in the more expensive trading pool. It was developed on the Rinkeby and Fantom testnets, then deployed on the Fantom mainnet.

June, 2021

Auto Leverage Farming Bot

In Defi, it is possible to use your liquidity provider tokens as collatoral to leverage against, to earn a higher yield. However, this risks liquidation. To safeguard against this, I created a bot in Web3 that monitors the current liquidation level of my leveraged position, and automatically deleverages when liquidation is close. This bot interacts with the Tarot protocol Solidity contracts on the Fantom blockchain.

VOARLA (Voice Operated Augmented Reality Logistics Application) is a hands-free pathfinding system, designed to guide the user to the goal. I was the sole designer and developer. Built in Unity for the Magic Leap One, the prototype was used to complete research into voice interaction with natural language understanding in AR. It utilizes IBM Watson Cloud speech services.

February, 2019

AR Workplace Induction Prototype

Aimed at reducing the need for supervised workplace inductions, I was the sole developer building the prototype. Built in Unity, the induction instructions were superimposed over the real-world physical drill press. It featured training videos and text instructions, activated by the AR wearers proximity to the drill press. Created in four days, the prototype was later demonstrated at Air NZ.

March - October, 2018

Te Papa Touchscreen Bird Game

With a target audience of five to twelve year olds, this game is currently on display at the Museum of New Zealand, Te Papa Tongarewa. The theme is native New Zealand and involves matching the correct bird to tree, to help grow a forest. I was the team leader, contributing to the management, design and development of the project.
